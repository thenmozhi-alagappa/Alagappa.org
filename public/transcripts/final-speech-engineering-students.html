Speeches of Alagappa Chettiar

Dr. Alagappa Chettiar – Last Speech, for the Students of Alagappa Chettiar College of Engineering and Technology

15th February 1957

Right throughout my life, it has never been my habit to take things lying down. I have always stood up for my rights, fought for them and pleaded for them. But now it has become the will of God that I should be confined to bed lying down for the last 8 months and God knows for how many months more. Friends, I am deeply overwhelmed with the elaborate arrangements that you have made and what is more, the great enthusiasm that you have shown in asking me to be present at least for a few moments before I leave for Madras.

As I first start speaking to you I must first tender my heartfelt thanks naturally to Providence but later immediately, the equivalence of Providence – that is, to my doctors who have been unremittingly attending on me day and night through thick and thin, irrespective and despite the great hardships that I have been causing them by making such undue demands on them. I am most grateful to them and I wish to publicly, in the presence of my fellow students, acknowledge the great debt that I owe to them and the great kindness that I have received from them and that I will continue to receive from them the rest of my life.

Friends, it was Lord Tennyson who sang, "My strength is as the strength of ten because my heart is pure." I may for a moment make an amendment to the great Tennyson and say that my happiness, despite my physical pain and unhappiness, is boundless, limitless – because my happiness does not consist naturally of my own happiness but my happiness consists in the happiness of the thousands of students, young and old, boy and girl, who study in our vast educational campus.

Friends, it is that thought that has been keeping me alive even during these terrible months, seven or eight months, and during the course of this foul disease to which I have fallen a victim. Friends, I do not propose to talk to you any more except to tell you that any individual credit that you take, or any happiness that comes to any one of you individually or collectively, I consider it as my own happiness – whether I know you individually or not. I think of this campus as one indivisible unit, both of students and staff, and I take such unmixed happiness from their own happiness, and this is what keeps me alive, as I told you a bit earlier.

Friends, I wish to convey both to you and to the other students of the campus, and to those who are not here, my sincere good wishes for personal happiness, not merely during the tenure of the few years that you will study in the campus but furthermore in the rest of your life, in the wider amphitheatre where you will play a part after you leave the college campus. God bless you is my sincere prayer, and I hope, God willing and if it is God's will, I will come back and see you; or in any event, I pray to God that everything will be well with you.

– Dr. RM. Alagappa Chettiar
15-2-1957
